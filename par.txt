The air in the convention center hung thick with the scent of stale coffee, burnt pizza, and pure, undiluted desperation. It was 3 AM, and the glow of four separate monitors illuminated the faces of the team huddled around a makeshift workstation: The Lexicon Erasers.

Their mission, audacious and utterly absurd, was to build an Artificial Intelligence capable of perfect linguistic deletion—an AI that could take any block of text and strip away every single word, leaving behind only the raw structure of grammar and syntax.

There were three members, each representing a different facet of the struggle.

There was Leo, the Architect. He was fueled by pure logic and caffeine, his fingers a blur across the keyboard as he wrestled with Python libraries and complex natural language processing algorithms.
